Two kernels, one recipe — live

The same parametric recipe (recipes/studio_wall.ir) evaluated by two engines that represent geometry in meaningfully different ways: an exact analytic B-rep (OCCT — a cylinder is a true surface) and a polyhedral mesh kernel (Manifold — a cylinder is N flat facets). Drag the sliders; watch the volumes, the measured divergence, and the match_cast verdicts respond. Everything below uses the same closed-form math the real kernels exhibit — verified against them by smoke_kernels.py.

What to notice

Boxes agree exactly, always. Prismatic geometry is exact in both worlds — the wall's Δ stays 0.000% no matter what you do. This is why the "shareable core" of AEC geometry is realistic to exchange parametrically.

The colonnade's Δ is pure representation difference. Nobody has a bug: drop N to 12 and the polyhedral kernel is honestly ~4.5% short of πr²h. The contract (ε) decides whether that counts as "the same shape." Faceting is the receiving kernel's lowering policy — the -ffast-math of geometry — which is why it lives on the slider and not in the recipe.

The pedestal never goes live. Its recipe uses geom.fillet, which needs B-rep edges; a mesh kernel has only triangles. It falls back to the baked oracle — frozen but correct, flagged not hidden. One file, three different honest outcomes: that is per-element degradation.

The window stays centered. Its x-position is the carried relation (wall_len − win_w)/2, recomputed on every kernel at every parameter change — the value was never baked. That single behavior is what IFC's DirectShape import cannot do, and it's the Relax "first-class symbolic shape" idea in one slider.
